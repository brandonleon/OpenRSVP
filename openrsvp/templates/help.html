OpenRSVP Help

Accountless events, magic links, and channel organization.

Creating Events

  • Channels are optional—events can stand alone or be grouped inside a channel. Pick a channel name that feels unique to your community if you want one.
  • Channel options appear as you type; selecting one reuses the existing channel. No login is required.
  • Private events remain hidden even when their channel is public.

Public vs Private Channels

Channels are lightweight collections of events:

  • Public channels show up on the homepage filter and have a public listing under /channel/<slug>.
  • Private channels never appear in the UI. Only people with the link can view them at /channel/p/<slug>.
  • Private channels do not make private events public—visibility is per-event.

Naming Tips & Warnings

  • Channel names are converted to slugs: letters are lowercased, accents are stripped to ASCII, and anything besides a-z/0-9 turns into a single hyphen (edges trimmed).
  • Channel slugs are derived from the name. Using common words like “party” or “friends” increases the odds of collisions.
  • We recommend including location or community hints (e.g., “san-diego-cigar-social”).
  • Private channels rely purely on the slug—treat them like a shared secret.

Safety & Privacy

  • No accounts or emails are stored. Keep your magic links private.
  • RSVP tokens are shown once after submission—save them if you’ll need to edit later.
  • Stale events and channels decay automatically; keep them active by sharing and visiting.

Need more? Reach out to the event owner directly—OpenRSVP never stores contact info.
